Now that it is over, it is time to do a thorough analysis of the results.

Baysian Analysis¶

For some reason I feel obligated to put this here $$ P(\theta|x) \sim P(x|\theta)P(\theta) $$ moving on

There are a few questions that I want to answer with this analysis 1) What is the chance, per attempt , that I make the paper ball into the trash 2) Was there any significant improvement over time

To answer this question it is best to model the data with a geometric distribution $$ P(x|\theta)=\theta(1-\theta)^{x-1} $$ Where $\theta$ is the success rate of a single throw and $x$ is the number of attempts untill a success occurs. In this formalism $x$ is our data plus 1 to include the trail that succeeded.

Optimistically I throught that I would make about 1/4 of all shots so I'll pick a prior with a mean around .25 with low power at high values. I'll pick a Beta distribution with $\alpha=1.5$ and $\beta=5$ as defined by pymc

I've also done more work than I needed to. The Beta distribution is the conjugate prior of the geometric distribution so we could have easily avoided all of this sampling nonsense and done the following.

In [12]:
prior_alpha=1.5
prior_beta=5
means=[]
stds=[]
for i in range(0,10):
    subset_data=data[i:i+5]+1 # get the sub window and add 1
    #look at wikipedia here https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Conjugate_prior
    post_alpha=prior_alpha+5
    post_beta=prior_beta+subset_data.sum()
    means.append(post_alpha/(post_alpha+post_beta))
    stds.append(np.sqrt((post_alpha*post_beta)/((post_alpha+post_beta+1)*(post_beta+post_alpha)**2)))

Disscusion and Future Work¶

After much too in-depth analysis It pains me to report that my paper ball throwing prowess is not only worse than I had thought, I also made no significant improvement over 2 years. There are many ways to put my performance into perspective, by far the worst is the fact that over 2 years I only successfully threw 15 paper balls into the garbage. While this was a fun exercise the experimental design was too simple to yield any truly interesting results, there were just too few variables in play. Now that I have moved to tate 201-08 I propose a new experiment that everyone can participate in! I have scribed a target onto the glass window (its dry erase, don't worry) for the toy dart gun that came with the office.
